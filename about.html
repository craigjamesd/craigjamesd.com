CJ Davitt is a New Jersey painter whose work is inseparable from the salt water that has shaped nearly every chapter of his life.

Growing up, his family summered in Lavallette on the shores of Barnegat Bay. Those early years — fishing for snappers with bobber rigs, crabbing on the family's old Dixie, watching the light shift over the open water — became the visual vocabulary he would later pour onto canvas.

"I started painting bobbers because, when I was young, that's what we did at the shore. We fished for snappers with bobber rigs in the bay."

Today, CJ lives near Sandy Hook with his wife and two kids, just a short walk from the water. By day he works as a physician assistant in orthopedic surgery — a demanding career that makes the studio all the more essential. Painting, like fishing, forces him to be completely present. It is his reset.

His transition from colored pencil illustration to oil painting began during the pandemic, when he had more time at home and a hunger for a new challenge. His technique places heavy emphasis on shadow, texture, and hyper-realism. He occasionally trades his brush for a palette knife, loading thick paint that dries with a sculptural surface — the bobbers in his paintings look like you could reach out and pick them up.

"I just paint the things I like because they have meaning to me. If people enjoy them or find meaning in my work, that's great."

Weathered bobbers. Bushels of blue crabs. Stormy open water. Fishermen in yellow slickers. Rope knots. Striped bass. Shark teeth. Every subject is a product of where CJ was raised and where he lives — the Jersey Shore, in all its battered, beautiful, salt-stained glory.

"Over a short time, Davitt has developed a unique style that makes his work easy to identify even without his signature."

— On The Water Magazine, January 2026

The Story
From Barnegat Bay to gallery walls
Early Years
Summers on Barnegat Bay
Fishing, crabbing, and long days on the family boat planted the seeds of a lifelong love of the coast.
Pre-2020
Colored Pencil & Illustration
Drawing designs for fishing friends — logos, hat artwork — built the technical foundation.
2020
First Oil on Canvas
The pandemic gave time and hunger for challenge. A misplaced brushstroke became a flock of seagulls. He kept them.
2021–23
Finding His Identity
The bobber paintings became his signature. Shadow, texture, hyper-realism — and a palette knife for sculptural depth.
2024–25
Gallery Recognition
Work placed in Frederick Gallery, Autumn Elise, and Noon Designs across the Jersey Shore.
2026
On The Water — Featured Artist
Selected as the featured Angling Artist in the 2026 On The Water Angler's Almanac.
