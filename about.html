About the Artist
CJ Davitt — Angling Artist

CJ Davitt is a New Jersey painter whose work is inseparable from the salt water that has shaped nearly every chapter of his life.

Growing up, his family summered in Lavallette on the shores of Barnegat Bay. Those early years — fishing for snappers with bobber rigs, crabbing on the family's old Dixie, watching the light shift over the open water — became the visual vocabulary he would later pour onto canvas.

"I started painting bobbers because, when I was young, that's what we did at the shore. We fished for snappers with bobber rigs in the bay."

Today, CJ lives near Sandy Hook with his wife and two kids. By day he works as a physician assistant in orthopedic surgery — a demanding career that makes the studio all the more essential. Painting, like fishing, forces him to be completely present.

His technique places heavy emphasis on shadow, texture, and hyper-realism. He occasionally trades his brush for a palette knife, loading thick paint that dries with a sculptural surface — the bobbers in his paintings look like you could reach out and pick them up.

"I just paint the things I like because they have meaning to me. If people enjoy them or find meaning in my work, that's great."

Weathered bobbers. Bushels of blue crabs. Stormy open water. Fishermen in yellow slickers. Every subject is a product of where CJ was raised and where he lives — the Jersey Shore, in all its battered, beautiful, salt-stained glory.
