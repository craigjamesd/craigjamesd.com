About the Artist

CJ Davitt —
Angling Artist

CJ Davitt is a New Jersey painter whose work is inseparable from the salt water that has shaped nearly every chapter of his life.

Growing up, his family summered in Lavallette on the shores of Barnegat Bay. Those early years — fishing for snappers with bobber rigs, crabbing on the family's old Dixie, watching the light shift over the open water — became the visual vocabulary he would later pour onto canvas.

"I started painting bobbers because, when I was young, that's what we did at the shore. We fished for snappers with bobber rigs in the bay."

Today, CJ lives near Sandy Hook with his wife and two kids, just a short walk from the water. By day he works as a physician's assistant in orthopedic surgery — a demanding career that makes the studio all the more essential. Painting, like fishing, forces him to be completely present. It is his reset.

His transition from colored pencil illustration to oil painting began during the pandemic. His technique places heavy emphasis on shadow, texture, and hyper-realism — often using a palette knife to build thick, tactile surfaces that make his subjects look like you could reach out and pick them up.

"I just paint the things I like because they have meaning to me. If people enjoy them or find meaning in my work, that's great."

Weathered bobbers. Bushels of blue crabs. Stormy open water. Fishermen in yellow slickers. Rope knots. Striped bass. Shark teeth. Every subject is a product of where CJ was raised and where he lives — the Jersey Shore, in all its battered, beautiful, salt-stained glory.

The Story

From Barnegat Bay to gallery walls

Early Years
Summers on Barnegat Bay
Growing up in New Jersey, CJ's family summered in Lavallette. Fishing for snappers, crabbing off the dock, and spending long days on the family boat planted the seeds of a lifelong love of the coast.
Pre-Pandemic
Colored Pencil & Illustration
Like his father, CJ developed a talent for illustration — drawing designs for fishing buddies to use on hats and boat logos. His hyperrealist pencil work built the foundation for everything to come.
2020
First Oil on Canvas
The pandemic gave him time and a hunger for challenge. A happy accident involving a misplaced white brushstroke became a flock of seagulls. He kept them — and kept painting.
2021–2023
Finding His Identity
The bobber paintings became CJ's signature. He developed a style heavy on shadow, texture, and depth — often using a palette knife to build thick surfaces that make his subjects pop off the canvas.
2024–2025
Gallery Recognition
Work placed in Frederick Gallery, Autumn Elise Gallery, and Noon Designs across the Jersey Shore. Collector response was immediate.
2026
On The Water — Featured Angling Artist
Selected as the featured Angling Artist in the 2026 On The Water Angler's Almanac — the Mid-Atlantic's premier fishing publication.

"Over a short time, Davitt has developed a unique style that makes his work easy to identify even without his signature."

— On The Water Magazine, January 2026
